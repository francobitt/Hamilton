# The Value of Urban Green Spaces

Parks and green spaces are among the most important assets a city can offer its residents. Research consistently shows that access to nature within urban environments produces measurable benefits — for individual health, the broader environment, and community life alike.

One of the most well-documented advantages is the effect on resident wellbeing. According to a 2022 WHO report, access to green space reduces stress markers by up to 30% in dense urban populations. For city dwellers contending with noise, congestion, and the pressures of daily life, the presence of a nearby park or tree-lined street can make a meaningful difference in mental health.

Green spaces also carry significant environmental value. A single mature tree absorbs approximately 48 pounds of CO2 per year, contributing to cleaner air across the city. Urban tree canopies further reduce surface temperatures by up to 8 degrees Fahrenheit, which lowers cooling costs for nearby buildings and helps cities manage the growing challenge of urban heat.

Beyond health and environment, green spaces strengthen the social fabric of neighborhoods. Community gardens and parks have been linked to lower crime rates, greater social cohesion, and higher property values in surrounding areas. Cities that invest in green infrastructure also report stronger civic engagement. In short, urban green spaces are not an amenity — they are an investment in the long-term vitality of the communities they serve.
